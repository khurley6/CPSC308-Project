Majors and Minors

Physics and Applied Mathematics

The Physics & Applied Mathematics major blends rigorous coursework in both physics and applied mathematics to give you a truly interdisciplinary education. You’ll develop strong analytical and problem-solving skills that prepare you for careers in aerospace, civil, electrical, or mechanical engineering—especially through Notre Dame’s Five-Year Dual Degree Engineering Program.

PAM Major Requirements
Computing and Applied Mathematics

This major combines core principles of computer science with advanced applied mathematics. You’ll learn to design algorithms, build software, and apply quantitative techniques to real-world problems—skills highly valued in technology-driven industries. It also aligns seamlessly with the Five-Year Dual Degree in Computer Science Engineering at Notre Dame.

CAM Major Requirements
Statistics and Actuarial Mathematics

One of only two women’s colleges to offer a SAM major, this program is fully recognized by the Society of Actuaries as a pre-actuarial curriculum. You’ll gain expertise in probability, statistical modeling, risk assessment, and financial mathematics—preparing you for careers in insurance, finance, consulting, and beyond.

SAM Major Requirements
Mathematics

Our flexible Mathematics major builds a deep foundation in pure and applied mathematics, equipping you for roles in industry, research, or graduate study. Choose the Teacher’s Option to earn the pedagogical training you need for licensure in secondary mathematics education. You can also combine this major with Notre Dame’s Five-Year Dual Degree in Engineering Program.

Math Major Requirements
Mathematics Teacher Concentration

Designed for future educators, this track provides both advanced mathematical content and best-practice teaching methods. You’ll complete coursework in curriculum design, instructional strategies, and classroom management—ensuring you graduate ready to lead high-school math classrooms.

MATT Major Requirements
Math Minors

Computer Science Minor: Gain hands-on experience with programming, data structures, and system design. Ideal for students who want practical coding skills alongside any major.

Math Minor: Deepen your quantitative toolkit with courses in calculus, algebra, and statistics. Perfect for majors in science, business, or the humanities who rely on strong analytical reasoning.
