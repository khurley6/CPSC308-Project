Study Abroad

Rome, Italy

This study abroad location is one of Saint Mary's most popular. Math Majors can spend a semester or a year in the heart of Italy, surrounded by 3000 year-old architecture and rich culture. This program also offers a variety of classes on top of Mathematics including Art, Philosophy, Religion, and Italian. Classes are taught at John Cabot University.

Program Overview
Budapest, Hungary

This study abroad program is designed specifially for students pursuing Mathematics. Budapest has a rich history in mathematics, where students will spend a semester taking "Combinatorics" and "Conjecture and Proof." Although prior experience in Hungarian is not required, courese will have the oppourtinity to take courses and to learn the language.

Program Overview
Maynoonth, Ireland

This progrm has been offered since 1977! Math majors will study at Maynoonth University and have various opportunities to travel Europe. Various courses that are offered include Mathematics, Drama, and Anthropology.

Program Overview
Cyprus-Nicosia

Cyprus is considered to be one of the safest places in the world. Studnts will study at the University of Nicosia, where over 14,500 students are hosted. Math Majors will be able to learn various mathematics-related skills such as Accounting, Finance, and Business skills.

Writing Proficiency Program

The Writing Proficiency Program is a program that students of all majors must participate in. Even as a math major, the purpose of this program is to help students become better writers. This include developing and explaining mathematical concepts through various papers and portfolios. Each Math major must complete a Basic W course, which is Saint Mary's writing requirement. Math majors must also complete "Advanced W" courses beginning their sophomore year, in which they will foster their skills for their senior comprehensive. Tutoring

In the heart of the Cushwa-Leighton Learning Commons is Saint Mary's very own Writing & Tutoring Center. Not only does the center have resources for math classes, but nearly all of the courses offered. Math Majors are strongly encouraged to utilize this resource. Also be sure to check with your professors if the course has a TA, and what their office hours are.
